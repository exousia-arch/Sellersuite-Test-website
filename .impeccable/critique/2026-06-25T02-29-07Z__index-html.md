## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment:** Does not read as obviously AI-made — the monospace readout layer, graph-paper hero, and "// comment" voice lines give it real personality the median SaaS landing lacks. BUT the brand spine (indigo→cyan gradient + Inter) is the single most-recognizable AI palette/font combo; the page is saved by its mitigations, not by the core palette choice. The features section is the most templated block: identical 4-card grid + rounded-square icon tiles + 01–04 numbers — three tells stacked.

**Deterministic scan (14 findings):** gradient-text ×4 (L67, L85, L117, L264), ai-color-palette ×5 (indigo/cyan, L67/85/117/249/264), overused-font ×2 (Inter, L27/33), numbered-section-markers ×1, em-dash-overuse ×1, design-system-color ×1 (L52). Detector agrees with the LLM read on the palette/font tells. Of the 4 gradient-text hits, 3 are sanctioned by DESIGN.md (wordmark ×2 + hero accent) = false positives against our own doctrine; **L117 (proof-band "0") is a real out-of-policy violation** of the Wordmark Exception Rule.

## What's Working
- **The mono readout layer** (eyebrows, KPIs, "// no OAuth" voice) is the page's signature and its strongest anti-generic move.
- **Privacy positioning is clear, repeated, and differentiated** — "100% in your browser, no upload, no login" lands immediately; the "0 files uploaded" stat is a clever proof.
- **Strong information hierarchy and copy discipline** — sections flow logically, headings are confident, FAQ is genuinely useful.

## Priority Issues

- **[P1] Dual co-equal CTAs dilute the primary action.** Hero, proof, nav, and closing all present "Try N-Gram free" AND "Request access" as equal-weight. The page hedges on what it wants. **Fix:** pick the free N-Gram as THE primary (top-of-funnel hook for a private beta), demote Request Access to a consistent secondary everywhere. → `/impeccable layout` or `/impeccable distill`
- **[P1] Brand palette is the textbook AI tell.** indigo→cyan + Inter is exactly the second-order category reflex ("SaaS tool, not-purple → indigo-cyan"). Mitigated by mono/grid/voice, but it's a strategic identity risk worth a conscious decision. **Fix:** either commit harder to a more ownable accent, or lean the mitigations even further (more mono surface, less gradient). → `/impeccable colorize` (strategic) or accept-and-document
- **[P2] Features section is the most templated block.** Identical card grid + rounded-square icon tiles + 01–04 numbers on four *parallel* tools (not a sequence). Three named tells stacked. **Fix:** drop the numbers (they imply order that doesn't exist), differentiate the cards, rethink the icon tiles. → `/impeccable layout`
- **[P2] Sub-AA contrast on muted text.** `text-slate-400` (#94a3b8 ≈ 2.9:1 on white) on the hero "// no OAuth" line and footer; `text-slate-500` on `slate-50` proof labels is ~4.4:1 (just under). **Fix:** bump muted body toward slate-600/700. → `/impeccable audit` then `/impeccable polish`
- **[P2] Mobile nav loses 4 of 6 links.** `hidden sm:inline` hides Tools / How / FAQ / N-Gram on mobile with no hamburger; mobile users get only Sign in + Request access. **Fix:** add a mobile menu or surface the free-tool link. → `/impeccable adapt`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan (First-Timer):** "Smart N-Gram" in the nav and hero with no one-line explanation — a first-time Amazon seller may not know what an n-gram is. Two CTAs create a "which do I click?" stall.

**Casey (Mobile):** Nav collapses to just Sign in + Request access; the free-tool hook (the thing you want them to try) disappears on phones. Hero CTAs stack fine, but the primary funnel link is hidden in the worst place.

**Skeptical Seller/Agency (project persona):** Privacy claims are strong and well-repeated, but there's zero third-party trust signal (no testimonial, logo, or named operator). Fine for private beta; a gap before public launch.

## Minor Observations
- Em-dash overuse in body copy is a subtle AI-writing tell; vary the punctuation.
- Feature-card numbers use `text-slate-300` (decorative, near-invisible) — if they stay, they're not earning their space.
- `L52` hardcoded slate grid color sits outside the token set (cosmetic).

## Questions to Consider
- If you could keep only one CTA above the fold, which earns the click — and does the rest of the page agree with that choice?
- The mono layer is what makes this page yours. What would happen if it carried *more* of the surface and the gradient carried less?
- Do four parallel tools need to look identical, or is one of them the hero the other three support?
